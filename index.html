Research

I am interested in bridging concepts from robotics, machine learning, and control theory to build robitic systems capable of safe and efficient interactions with humans and the environment. I am currently exploring proprioceptive impedance control to achieve dynamic interations with a planar tendon-driven continuum robot.

Before that, I researhed the risk of side channel attack on head mounted consumer devices with IMU from audio and speech processing deep learning models. I have also worked on parameter estimation to create airctaft stall model that facilitate improved simulation training.

Proprioceptive Impedance Control of a Planar Tendon-Driven Continuum Robot
Jimmy Chengnan Shentu, Reinhard M. Grassmann, Jessica Burgner-Kahrs
Manuscript in preparation, 2022

Investigate and implement an impedance controller for a planar continuum robot using the actuation units, to achieve dynamic interactions with the environment while ensuring safety.
One Actuation Unit to Create Them All – Toward Torque-Controlled Continuum Robots
Reinhard M. Grassmann, Jimmy Chengnan Shentu, Jessica Burgner-Kahrs
Manuscript in preparation, 2022

This work seeks to develop an open-source, moduler actuation unit for easier continuum robot prototyping, making the prototype development more efficient in the research process.
