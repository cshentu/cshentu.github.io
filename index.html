Research

I am interested in bridging concepts from robotics, machine learning, and control theory to build robotic systems capable of safe and efficient interactions with humans and the environment. I am currently exploring proprioceptive impedance control to achieve dynamic interations with a planar tendon-driven continuum robot.

Before that, I researched the risk of side channel attack on head mounted consumer devices with IMU from audio and speech processing deep learning models. I have also worked on parameter estimation to create airctaft stall models that facilitate improved simulation for pilot training.

Proprioceptive Impedance Control of a Planar Tendon-Driven Continuum Robot
Jimmy Chengnan Shentu, Reinhard M. Grassmann, Jessica Burgner-Kahrs
Manuscript in preparation, 2022, flagship robotics conference

In this work, I aim to investigate and implement an impedance controller for a planar continuum robot using the actuation units designed in the project below, to achieve dynamic interactions with the environment while ensuring safety.
Toward Torque-Controlled Continuum Robots
Reinhard M. Grassmann, Jimmy Chengnan Shentu, Taqieldin Hamoda, Jessica Burgner-Kahrs
Manuscript in preparation, 2022, major robotics journal

This work seeks to develop an open-source, moduler actuation unit for easier continuum robot prototyping, making the prototype development more efficient in the research process.
Risk of Side Channel Attack on Head Mounted Devices
Supervised by Prof. Xin Yi, Tsinghua University
May - Sep, 2021

This work seeks to investigate the risk of side channel attack on head mounted consumer devices, such as VR headsets and smart-glasses, through inertial measurement unit (IMU) by recovering speech or motion information. I identified several machine learning models that could eavesdrop on device userswithout microphone access and discussed how to defend such attacks effectively.
Mixed Parameter Estimationto of Full Stall Aircraft Model
Supervised by Prof. Peter Grant, University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies
May - Aug, 2019

This project is motivatedby aircraft stall being one of the leading causes of commercial aircraft accidents. The outcome is an improved aircraft flight model suitable for professional simulation training.
